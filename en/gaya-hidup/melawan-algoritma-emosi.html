Jangan Mau Dipermainkan Algoritma: Why Anger is Commodified on Social Media

Stop! Jangan Mau Dipermainkan Algoritma

This essay titled **"Jangan Mau Dipermainkan Algoritma"** is a piercing reflection on our bitter digital realities. Using the analogy of a sweetened coffee without sugar, the author invites us to recognize the biggest irony of modern era: we, as humans, are so passive-aggressive to be guided by the artificial intelligence systems of a few tech giants, who care not for our well-being, but only for one thing: **the duration of our eyes on the screen.**

1: Commodification of Emotions and Illusion of Control

The author bluntly states that algorithms have transformed from neutral tools to the **"shady black market traders"** that know exactly what we seek—what we want, not what we need, but what quickly stirs our emotions.

  • **Memicu Kemarahan:** Content that triggers conflicts and anger makes us react faster and, most importantly, persists in the app.
  • **Memeras Emosi:** Fear, disgust, and anxiety are exploited to become valuable commodities.
  • **Menjual Durasi:** Their primary goal is to maximize **the duration of users in the app**, as anger and inner disharmony have high value in the form of ads.

Despite Control Already Being Taken

This essay hits the belief of many adults who feel strong, "I just *scroll*, control is in my hands." The author reminds us that control has already been taken. This is not a conspiracy theory, but a deliberately designed business mechanism to weaken vigilance and maximize irritation. The phone is not a neutral entity; every decision on the screen is made for the benefit of the company, not to make us vigilant or peaceful.

2: Parents and Children: The Exhausted Target

This analysis focuses on the vulnerabilities of parents, a crucial point that is often overlooked.

Exhausted Parents are the Best Targets

Parents are not seen as easy targets because they are foolish, but because they are **exhausted**. After a full day of work, taking care of children, and worrying about debts, they enter the digital space in a psychologically vulnerable state:

  • False news feels more convincing than official explanations.
  • Glamorous videos are more appealing than a conversation with a child.

Guarding the 'Soft' Mind

The author warns sternly: if adults are ensnared by a machine knowing precisely when we are hungry, sad, bored, or vulnerable, **even more so for children whose minds are still as soft as freshly baked bread.** We focus on protecting children from the outside world, but forget that the greatest danger has already moved to our own small screens in our hands.

3: Self-Reflection: Introspection to Protect

The solution proposed by the author is simple, yet fundamental and powerful:

**Maintaining anger means maintaining righteousness.** And maintaining righteousness means protecting the family from the world that is deliberately made loud and filled with conflict.
