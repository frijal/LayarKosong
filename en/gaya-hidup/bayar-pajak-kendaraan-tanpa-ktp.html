Syarat Resmi Bayar Pajak Kendaraan Bermotor

Paying PKB is a mandatory requirement for obtaining a yearly vehicle inspection permit (STNK). Without a valid proof of payment, the STNK is considered invalid and the vehicle is illegal on the road.

Based on Regulation of the Police No. 7 of 2021 concerning Vehicle Registration and Identification, the documents that must be submitted when paying PKB (Road Tax) are:

  • - Original STNK (Vehicle Tax Receipt)
  • - Original KTP of the vehicle owner whose name is listed on the STNK
  • - Motor Vehicle Title Deed (BPKB)- (only for paying a five-year tax or changing a plate)
  • - Cash or non-cash payment method available

Why is Original KTP Matching Original STNK Required?

The KTP serves as a formal identification of a citizen. Matching the KTP with the STNK aims to ensure that the vehicle owner or a valid representative is present. This is important to prevent identity theft, document forgery, and to ensure that registration data always matches. In the digital police system, each transaction will be recorded and verified in real-time. If the KTP does not match, the system will reject processing.

Why Does the "KTP Snatching" Practice and Black Market Services Still Exist?

The term "snatching KTP" refers to the practice of using the identity of another person (usually by borrowing a KTP) or exploiting black market services to pay tax despite an original KTP not matching. This practice is prevalent due to several factors.

Factors of Bureaucracy and Public Perception

Pertama, there are still many vehicles that have changed hands multiple times but have not had their ownership records updated. The new owner is simply too lazy to maintain because it is considered too complicated and expensive. Second, the perception that long and complicated queues at the Samsat make buying a "fast lane" solution appealing. Third, there is a lack of understanding that reversing vehicle ownership can be done simultaneously with tax payments. Fourth, there are dishonest individuals taking advantage of the situation by offering "fast lane" services at additional costs.

Risks of Using a Fast Lane

Using a fast lane or "fast lane" to update your KTP is like walking on the edge of a cliff. Some of the risks include:

  • **Official records not recorded:** The staff may process manually, but the data does not enter the ERI (Electronic Registration and Identification) system. As a result, when you check your vehicle registration online, your vehicle is not listed.
  • **Your vehicle can be blocked:** If inconsistencies are found, the police have the right to detain or block your vehicle registration.
  • **Criminal liability:** Article 263 of the Criminal Code (KUHP) could be applied if you use a fake KTP or identity without authorization.
  • **Financial loss:** Fast lane operators usually charge a high fee without any guarantee of success.

If someone tries to use a go-between and it turns out that the data entered is invalid, then the paid STNK can have a "inactive" or "questionable" status. As a result, the vehicle can be fined and the STNK can be confiscated.

**Safe and Legal Vehicle Tax Payment Methods**

**Official and Digital Payment Options**

Currently, the government provides many official payment channels:

  • - Central Vehicle Administration Office (Korlantas) and Samsat branches at malls.
  • - Samsat Mobile (schedule can be checked online).
  • - Samsat Drive-Through (fast without leaving).
  • - SIGNAL (National Samsat Digital) app and digital wallets (GoPay, OVO, LinkAja) in some areas.

For more detailed information and official app links, you can access throughOfficial website of the Policeor Samsat Korlantas.

**How to Avoid Denial**

  • Make sure your KTP matches the name on the Vehicle Inspection Certificate (STNK).
  • If the car is bought from someone else, promptly apply for a name change (BBN II) along with annual tax payment.
  • If the KTP is lost, first go to Dukcapil and use a temporary letter of identification that is still valid.
  • Never leave the original KTP with a middleman or unknown party.

**Frequently Asked Questions about Vehicle Motor Tax Payment Without KTP**

❓ Is it possible to pay tax without the original KTP?
By official procedure, using a genuine KTP that matches the STNK is mandatory. Without it, staff have the right to deny.
❓ Is paying through a middleman guaranteed to be safe?
There is no guarantee. Instead, there is a risk that the vehicle's license plate will be invalid, data will not be entered into the system, and even criminal charges.
❓ What is the ERI system?
Electronic Registration and Identification, the national digital system created by Korlantas that records data for vehicle owners and vehicles.
❓ What if the vehicle has not been renewed yet?
Do the vehicle registration renewal at the Samsat immediately. The process can be combined with paying annual taxes.
❓ Can the license plate be blocked if it is known to be used by a middleman?
The police have the right to block data if administrative irregularities are found.
❓ Why do people still use middlemen?
Because of lack of information, fear of complexity, or time pressure. However, now there are many official fast and reliable services available.
