Hi fellow Empty Screen readers and all the asphalt warriors wherever you are. 🇮🇩

Sixteen years. That's not a short time, folks. Imagine, children born when Gojek or Grab first appeared might now be in high school, or even high school freshmen. They're already teenagers, preparing to adulthood. But strangely, the legal status of millions of their parents who work day and night as Ojol drivers remains "infant." They're crawling, vulnerable, and their form is unclear.

We often hear slogans like "Nation's Pride" or "Digital Export Cash Cow," but it feels like hollow rhetoric. In reality? Ojol continues to operate within the vast legal vacuum. There are laws, but they're fragile. There are ministers, but they seem powerless.

Hierarchical law in Indonesia places regulations far above ministerial orders (Permen). Decree of the Ministry of Transportation (Permenhub) is easily swayed, lacking the teeth to bite the giant tech giants whose company valuations are bigger than a provincial budget.

"MINISTRY OF TRANSPORTATION, MINISTER'S ORDERS ARE NOT ENOUGH TO PROTECT MILLIONS OF DRIVERS. FARES THEMSELVES CANNOT END INJUSTICE."

The problem with ride-hailing is complex, not just about the per-kilometer fare. It's about workplace safety guarantees, pension security, data protection, algorithm transparency, and workers' collective rights. All of these need **"REGULATIONS, NOT TEMPORARY ORDERS."**

  1. What's the Risk Without Laws? ("Why")
  2. Why do we fight so hard? Let's look at the real-life situation on the streets:
  3. **Workplace Accidents:** If a driver gets injured while on the job, they often get treated as a personal mishap. The BPKR (Social Security Agency) is often paid for individually, whereas it should be *co-shared* with the app operator.

**Price War:** App Operator A and B wage promotions war, at the expense of whom? Drivers whose incomes are further squeezed.

**Unilateral Suspension:** Account suspension = Kitchen goes silent. The appeal process is difficult, often not transparent. Drivers lack legal protection to challenge the decisions of the machine.

That's why we shout: **"WE DEMAND JUSTICE, NOT PITY."** Ride-hailing doesn't ask for donations; ride-hailing just asks for fair rules. That's all.

  • **What (Apa):** Quickly submit the Online Taxi Bill/Gig Economy Worker Bill to the Priority Legislative Agenda.
  • **Who (Siapa):** Involve original driver representatives, not company-created organizations.
  • **When (Kapan):** NOW. 16 years have been too long. Don't wait for another big demonstration.
  • **Where (Di mana):** National applicability, from Sabang to Merauke. Don't have different rules for different cities.
  • **Why (Kenapa):** Article 5 of the Pancasila: Social Justice for all of Indonesia's people (including Ojol drivers).
  • **How (Bagaimana):** Redefine the partnership status. Force the app companies to be transparent about cuts and bonuses.
