Di the dawn, around 04:00 to 05:30 in the morning, the human body is actually undergoing a massive transition. Modern medicine refers to this as the **Circadian Rhythm** phase. This is the internal biological clock that regulates when you should wake up, sleep, and when your bodily organs should work at their peak.

"Science proves that at sunrise, there is a natural increase in cortisol levels. Cortisol is often called the stress hormone, but in the morning, it acts as the natural 'fuel' that keeps you alert and prepared for the world without feeling overly tired."

Why is Morning Air So Special for the Lungs? 🫁

Have you ever felt that your breath was heavier when you wake up in the morning? It’s not just a feeling you have. Clinically, the air during the dawn contains extremely pure ozone (O3) and oxygen levels that haven’t been contaminated by vehicle exhaust or industrial smoke. The ozone in the morning also has the property of helping to reduce inflammation in the respiratory system.

This pure air enters the alveoli of the lungs, cleaning out the leftover carbon dioxide from the night, and making your blood rich with fresh oxygen. This oxygen-rich blood is then pumped to the brain, preventing you from experiencing brain fog (a condition where you have difficulty concentrating) in the afternoon and evening. 🧠💧
