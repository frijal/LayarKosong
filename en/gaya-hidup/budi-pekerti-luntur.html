Budi Pekerti yang Luntur: We're Losing Our Morals or Courage?

Before we go further, we need to agree on its definition first. Budi pekerti isn't just about saying "excuse me" when passing by a parent or knowing table manners. That's only the surface. True budi pekerti is moral integrity in real actions. What does this mean? It's about consistency between the values we hold in our hearts and the public behaviors we display.

Kalau kita pakai standar ini, ya nggak heran kalau banyak dari kita merasa gelisah. Kita melihat lanskap publik kita yang sekarang penuh dengan polarisasi yang tajam banget. Ujaran kebencian bahkan sudah jadi komoditas politik yang laku keras. Hoaks? Wah, itu mah nyebarnya lebih cepet daripada klarifikasinya sendiri. 🏃💨

If we use this standard, it's no wonder many of us feel uneasy. We see the current public landscape full of sharp polarization. Hate speech has become a political commodity. Hoaxes? Well, they spread faster than they can be clarified. 🏃💨

Religiusitas vs Etika Publik 🏛️

"A person might appear very religious symbolically, with strict religious practices and polite speech, but at the same time, they can be extremely permissive towards corruption just because the perpetrators are in the 'one group.' This isn't just a matter of etiquette crisis, this is the crisis of integrity."

The issue is that we often confuse **morality with ritual**. However, public morality demands more than just regularly performing rituals. It requires bravery to be honest, full responsibility for one’s actions, and consistency in not “looking the other way” at rules. While we might have the values of the Pancasila ingrained in our heads, the question is: have values like "Humanity and Civilization" become a *habitus* or a deeply ingrained habit? Or are they only a decorative feature in textbooks? 📕

Algorithms: A New Enemy of Morality? 📱

What actually evaporates isn’t the moral teachings themselves—since these still exist and continue to be taught in schools or places of worship. What fades is the **culture of shame and social bravery**. In the past, our reputation in the eyes of the community or neighbors acted as a strong ethical control. People felt ashamed to do wrong because they would be criticized by their surroundings.

Now? The digital world, through its algorithms, often gives rewards (incentives) for extreme actions. Content that is full of complaints, criticisms, or sensationalist material spreads much faster than rational and calm arguments. In such an ecosystem, integrity ultimately loses its prominence to image management or *impression*.

Challenges in Education and Moral Agency 🎓

Here is the translation with the text and HTML tags preserved:

Akibatnya, banyak orang tumbuh jadi pribadi yang saleh secara personal (baik buat dirinya sendiri), tapi pasif secara sosial. Di sinilah pentingnya *agency moral*. Budi pekerti itu butuh nyali buat bersuara pas ada ketidakadilan. Tanpa keberanian buat bertindak, moralitas itu cuma jadi dekorasi yang nggak ada gunanya.

Dunia education also has a big challenge. For a long time, moral education has been only about rote memorization of norms. We are taught what is right and wrong theoretically, but rarely are we encouraged to discuss real-world **moral dilemmas**. For example: what if there are conflicting interests? How do you have the courage to go against the majority if the majority is wrong? 🌊

As a result, many people grow into individuals who are personally righteous (for themselves), but socially passive. This is where the importance of **moral agency** comes in. One’s character needs the courage to speak out when faced with injustice. Without the courage to act, morality is just decorative and useless.

The Gut and Top-down Example 🍞

We also need to be realistic. We can't just blame individuals. There are structural factors like economic disparities and job instability. When people feel unsafe economically, they tend to be pragmatic. Idealistic values often get displaced by survival concerns. That's why calls to morality will sound hollow if the system itself is not improved.

So, is our moral compass fading? Perhaps it's more accurate to say that we're in the midst of a massive social transformation, but not matched with appropriate ethical updates yet. We need a new definition of moral compass for the 21st century:

  • Preserve our image, but **care about the social impact** of every post we make.
  • Be polite, but **be truthful in presenting data**.
  • Follow our religious duties, but **be fair in making policies**.

Without strong public ethics, our democracy will be fragile. Without collective integrity, laws will just be a tool for the powerful. Our moral compass is not a legacy that automatically passes down. It needs to be defined, practiced, and maintained every day.

**A question for us all to consider:**
Do we still have the courage to promote truth when it's unpopular, unpopular, and not beneficial to our positions?

If we're still uncertain about that answer, then the problem isn't technology or the younger generation. The problem is within us, more inclined to complain about others' morals than to be a concrete example of our own. Let's start from small things, from ourselves, now! 💪✨
