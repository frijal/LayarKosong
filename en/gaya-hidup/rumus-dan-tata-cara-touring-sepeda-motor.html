This section covers formulas and official signaling codes for bikers, providing guidelines for effective driving in motor convoys, as per Mabes Polri directives. For a safe and unified command during convoy travel, it's essential to use rules and convoy protocols, which must be understood by all bikers.

Touring in motor vehicles together is one of the most anticipated activities for bikers. This activity involves **driving to distant places** while enjoying companionship and scenery. 🛣️

To ensure a safe and coordinated journey, using rules or guidelines in collective driving, such as signaling codes, is **essential for bikers**. The goal is for the journey to proceed smoothly and for all convoys to arrive safely at their destination. 🙏

Untuk memastikan perjalanan aman dan lebih menyatukan komando, maka sangat perlu digunakan aturan atau tata tertib dalam berkendara berjamaah atau touring konvoi. Penggunaan aturan, baik itu kode-kode serta tata cara dalam berkonvoi, **wajib dipahami oleh para bikers**.

Tujuannya jelas: Agar perjalanan dapat berjalan lancar dan semua rombongan tiba di lokasi dengan selamat. 🙏

**A Few Notes:** In touring, the Road Captain is the main determinant of formation and speed. Visual communication using hand signals is of utmost importance, especially when sounds of horns or radio communication are not clearly audible. Understanding these codes makes the convoy move as a professional unit, not just a group of motorcycles hindering traffic.

**Formation During Touring**

**Hand Signals (Parade Code)**

**01 - STOP (STOP):** Arms bent downward, palm facing the rear. This is the emergency code used by the sweeper or Road Captain when sudden danger is present.

**02 - SINGLE LINE FORMATION:** Arms and thumb extended straight upward. This formation is used on narrow roads or when passing large vehicles.

**03 - TURN ON THE LIGHTS:** Fingers and thumb straight, hands open and closed. This code is important when entering foggy areas or near sunset.

**04 - SLOW DOWN:** Arm extended horizontally downward, palm facing downward, a back-and-forth motion. Usually given before entering dense areas or sharp turns.

**05 - DOUBLE LINE FORMATION:** Arms with thumb and middle finger extended straight upward. The most common Zig-Zag formation used on wide roads, providing safe spacing between motorcycles.

**06 - **ISI BBM (BENSIN)**:** The index finger pointing towards the motorcycle tank. This signal is mandatory before the fuel is completely exhausted, so that everyone can fill up together.

**07 - **PERCEPAT JALAN:****: The forearm is straight, moving upwards, the palms are facing upwards. Used when entering a toll road or a deserted straight road.

**08 - **JALAN RAWAN BAHAYA:****: On the right, the right foot points downwards; on the left, the left hand points downwards. Indicates a large hole, oil spill, or dangerous item on the side.

**09 - **BERHENTI DAN ISTIRAHAT:****: The fingers are clenched, the thumb pointed towards the helmet mouth. This is the most liked signal by all members, it's time for a break and to stretch!**10 - **GANTI MEMIMPIN/PINDAH KEDEPAN:****: The forearm is at 45 degrees, the palms facing forward, the index finger pointing and swinging from behind to the shoulder. Usually given to a sweeper or a member considered capable of assisting the Road Captain.

**11 - **GANTI POSISI AGAR SEDIKIT MERUNDUK:****: The palms are pressed on the helmet with open palms downwards. This is usually done to reduce aerodynamic resistance at high speeds (aerodynamic).

**12 - **MENYARANKAN BERHENTI UNTUK ISTIRAHAT:****: The left arm is straight at 45 degrees, the palm clenched and moving up and down in short swings. Similar to the signal 09, but more pronounced to seek a place for a rest urgently.

**12 - MENYARANKAN BERHENTI UNTUK ISTIRAHAT**: Tangan kiri lurus ke samping 45 derajat, tangan mengepal dan mengayun pendek naik turun. Mirip kode 09, tapi lebih menekan untuk segera mencari tempat istirahat.

**13 - IKUTI SAYA**: Arm straight up, directly above the shoulder, palms open facing forward. The most commonly used Road Captain signal when exiting rest areas or intersections.

**14 - BERHASIL SAMPAI TUJUAN**: Arm straight out to the side, just below the shoulder, index finger straight, waving forward past the helmet. The most beautiful signal, indicating a successful touring mission! 🎉

Additional Signal: Left Hand, Foot, and Horn

In addition to the formal hand signals above, there are also practical signals commonly used by the Indonesian motor community. These signals serve as quick confirmations and more specific hazard notifications. Here's some additional knowledge from the PR Division of the National Police Command:

Hand Code (Gunakan hanya Tangan Kiri)

  • Hand Signals (Use Only the Left Hand)
  • **Thumb Up** = confirm ready to depart; or a brotherhood greeting. 👍
  • **One Finger** = form a single file convoy.
  • **Two Fingers** = form a two-file convoy.
  • **Five Fingers** = disperse the convoy temporarily to rejoin after passing through a dangerous area (e.g., severe traffic jam).
  • **Clapped Fingers** = ready to stop (only for stop points).

**Pointing in a Direction** = ready to turn in the direction pointed.

  • Foot Signals (Foot Signals)
  • **Lower Left Foot** = indicating a hazard or hole on the left.
  • **Lower Right Foot** = indicating a hazard or hole on the right.

Horn Code (Kode Klakson)

  • **Long beep** = confirmation of readiness to depart (only for sweeper); signal that the convoy has broken (only for sweeper); signal that the convoy is back together after breaking (only for sweeper).
  • **Repeated short beeps** = emergency stop request or there's an issue within the convoy. 🚨
  • **Two short beeps** = brotherhood/greeting or a quick thank you.

Ah, let's talk about the convoy etiquette like this, it's obligatory in motor touring. However, in everyday driving, this ethical practice can also be implemented, particularly in the **maintenance of a safe distance** from the vehicle in front.

If, at any time, the vehicle in front suddenly brakes, by maintaining the correct distance (Zig-Zag formation), you won't be involved in a collision and won't be hit as well. Remember, Bro & Sister, *Safety is not just a slogan, it's a way of life!* Always prioritize safety!
