👋 Here are some people who like to explode when they're angry? Or who easily get triggered and shout or break things when they get upset? Don't worry, you're not alone. Anger is a natural human emotion, a part of our human nature given to us by God. But have you ever considered that it might actually have some serious negative consequences as well?What are the dangers of this habit of getting angry?Ternyata, the effects are not just regretting afterward, but also can cause many serious issues, like damaging relationships with loved ones, losing wealth, and even destroying our own physical health!

Let's thoroughly examine the dangers of anger, from both the perspective of Islamic comfort and the logical viewpoint of medicine. Hopefully after reading this, we'll be more motivated to manage our anger (anger management) better! Let's not let our fleeting emotions destroy our long-term future. 💪✨

Here are some dangers of anger according to Islamic perspective:

In Islam, anger is often likened to a flame that is thrown into a human's heart. The heat, burns, and destroys. The Prophet ﷺ and our wise scholars have repeatedly warned about the hidden dangers of this. It's not to scare the weak, but to show their love and care so they may be safe in this world and the hereafter. Let's examine the points:

1. Mengundang Murka Allah

1. **Summoning Allah's Wrath**

The Prophet ﷺ said,
"Indeed, strength is not shown through power, but rather through strength in restraining oneself when angry."
(Trusted by Al Bukhari & Muslim)

Wah, turns out the definition of "the strongest" isn't just about having strong muscles or being good at fighting, but rather about having iron-like mental strength to control our emotions when they are at their peak. This is crucial, because someone who can't control their anger is a sign that their faith is weak. And we all know, when faith is weak and we follow our base desires (ego) instead, it can invite Allah's wrath. So, every time you feel like getting angry, take a deep breath and remember the Prophet's advice! 🙏

2. **The Gateway to Satan**

Allah said,

"Indeed, Shaytan aims to sow discord and hatred among you."
(al Māidah: 91)

Scholars explain that anger is one of the pathways for Satan to harm humans. Ibnul Qayyim even emphatically said,"غضب الإنسان نار يوقدها الشيطان"Anger is like an incandescent flame fueled by the devil. Let's imagine, when we get angry, it feels like the devil has prepared a VIP red carpet leading directly into our hearts, provoking us into doing foolish and wicked things. Let us not become the devil's plaything! Let us guard our hearts from the devil's misleading whispers. 😈

"Anger of man is a fire kindled by the devil"

3. Wipe Out Good Deeds

The Prophet ﷺ said,
"Anger is from the devil."
"Anger is from the devil" (Hadis reported by Ahmad)

The devil's main mission is to make us lose our good deeds. Anger is one of its deadliest weapons. When angry, people tend to say harsh things, hurt others' hearts, and commit sins without realizing it. From Mu'adz radhiyallahu 'anhu, he said,"ما شيء أفسد للأعمال من الغضب""The worst thing that destroys deeds is anger." So, after we've put in all the effort in prayer, charity, and good deeds, let's not let our investments be wasted just because of one moment of impulsive anger! It's such a waste, isn't it?

"Nothing destroys deeds more than anger"

**"Who can restrain their anger when they are capable of expressing it? Then Allah will summon them to the congregation of the created beings and give them a maid servant as they desire." (Sunan Abi Dawud)**

1. Penyakit Kardiovaskular (Jantung & Pembuluh Darah)

*Subhanallah! How great is the *reward* for someone who can restrain their anger. Its meaning is clear: the person who can restrain their anger will be promised paradise and honor on the Day of Judgment. On the contrary, someone who allows themselves to be overwhelmed by anger may miss out on the bounty of paradise. Who wouldn’t want such a reward from Allah? Let us practice restraining ourselves, even if it is difficult; the reward is paradise, my dear! 😇*

2. Hipertensi (Tekanan Darah Tinggi)

Same as the previous point, repeated and out-of-control anger can keep your blood pressure high chronically. This condition, known as *the Silent Killer*, can often have no symptoms but the effects can be fatal. 🔈

3. Inflammation (Inflammation)

Oxidative stress caused by anger triggers the body's inflammatory response. This chronic inflammation is the root cause of many serious diseases of the *now*, including rheumatoid arthritis, type 2 diabetes, accelerating cellular aging, and stimulating various types of cancer. So anger leads to early aging and illness! 🔥

4. Weakened Immune System

The constant release of stress hormones (cortisol) during anger weakens the body's immune system. The result? You become more susceptible to illnesses, catching colds easily, and wounds take longer to heal. The body's defense is compromised due to one's own emotions. 🦠

5. Digestive Issues Arise

Have you heard of the term "stomachache due to stress"? This is real. There's a strong connection between the brain and gut (*gut-brain axis*). Anger can disrupt the digestive system, causing heartburn, nausea, stomach pain, diarrhea, or even worsening conditions like GERD, stomach ulcers, and IBS (Irritable Bowel Syndrome). Meals become unpleasant, sleep becomes restless. 🤢

7. Muscle Pain and Tension

When angry, we unconsciously clench our fists or grind our teeth. The muscles throughout the body tense up, ready for a "fight". If this happens repeatedly, it can lead to tension headaches (achy head), migraines, stiff neck, sore shoulders, and back pain. It feels like your whole body is exhausted! 🤕

Amarah kronis bisa memengaruhi bagian otak (prefrontal cortex) yang mengatur emosi dan pengambilan keputusan logis. Ini bisa membuatmu jadi lebih impulsif, agresif, sulit berkonsentrasi, dan susah mikir jernih. Otak jadi "berkabut" terus isinya emosi doang. 🤯

8. Sleep Disturbances

Orang who cannot manage stress and anger often seek an instant escape, which is wrong. Some express it by smoking cigarettes, drinking alcohol, *emotional eating* (overeating), or speeding on the road. This obviously adds a long list of new health problems. 🍔🍾

Wow, turns out how dangerous anger is, super many and complex! From major sins threatening the afterlife to deadly diseases threatening life. So, if you still like to get angry, let's start now by learning to manage emotions (*Anger Management*).

There are many ways to *cool down* when emotions peak. The Prophet taught us to perform wudu (because water quenches the devil's fire), recite Taluudz, change body posture (from standing to sitting, or lying down), and even just sit quietly to avoid saying the wrong thing. If needed, don't hesitate to confide to a professional. Remember, taking care of the heart is very important for both this world and the afterlife.

May Allah continue to guide and protect us all so that we become patient, kind-hearted individuals who can control their anger. Amen, Lord of the Universe. Keep up the good work as a good person! 🙏😊
