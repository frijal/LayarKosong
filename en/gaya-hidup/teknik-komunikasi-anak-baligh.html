By: **Ustazah Yanti Tanjung**Hello, amazing parents!

Parenting an adolescent, or in Islamic terms, a **baligh** child, often makes us as parents feel like we need to "start from scratch" anew. Why? Because the child who used to be obedient is now starting to have their own arguments. The child who used to be pampered is now starting to value their privacy. This is a crucial transitional period.

Who and What Are the Role Models? (Who & What)

In Islamic parenting, we have the highest standard (*gold standard*) in communicating with adolescent children. Who else is this but **Prophet Ibrahim AS** during his dialogue with his son, **Prophet Ismail AS**. This dialogue is not just any ordinary conversation; it is recorded in the Quran in Surah Al-Asr verse 102. This is a high-level communication technique when a child reaches the age of *as-sa'ya* (able to try independently). Menghadapi anak yang mulai beranjak remaja atau dalam Islam disebut usia **Baligh**, seringkali membuat kita sebagai orang tua merasa perlu "belajar lagi" dari nol. Kenapa? Karena anak yang dulu penurut, kini mulai punya argumen sendiri. Anak yang dulu manja, sekarang mulai menjaga privasi. Inilah masa transisi yang krusial.

Siapa yang Menjadi Teladan Kita? (Who & What)

Dalam dunia parenting Islami, kita punya standar emas (*gold standard*) dalam berkomunikasi dengan anak baligh. Siapa lagi kalau bukan **Nabi Ibrahim AS** saat berdialog dengan putranya, **Ismail AS**. Dialog ini bukan sekadar obrolan biasa, tapi diabadikan oleh Allah SWT dalam Al-Qur'an Surat Ash-Shaffat ayat 102. Ini adalah teknik komunikasi tingkat tinggi saat seorang anak sudah mencapai usia *as-sa'ya* (mampu berusaha sendiri).

"Maka tatkala anak itu sampai (pada umur sanggup) berusaha bersama-sama Ibrahim, Ibrahim berkata: 'Hai anakku, sesungguhnya aku melihat dalam mimpi bahwa aku menyembelihmu. Maka pikirkanlah apa pendapatmu!'"

Bayangkan, perintah menyembelih anak adalah perintah yang sangat berat. Tapi perhatikan cara Nabi Ibrahim menyampaikannya: *"Fanzhur madza tara"* , Maka pikinlah apa pendapatmu! Beliau tidak langsung memaksa, tapi mengajak berdiskusi. Ini adalah kunci utama komunikasi kepada anak yang sudah baligh.

Kapan dan Di Mana Transisi Ini Terjadi? (When & Where)

Transisi ini terjadi tepat saat anak memasuki usia baligh. Di mana pun mereka berada, di rumah, sekolah, atau lingkungan sosial, pola pikir mereka sedang berubah. Menurut para ahli tafsir seperti **Quraish Shihab** dan **Ibnu Katsir**, usia ini ditandai dengan kemampuan anak untuk berjalan bersama ayahnya, bekerja, dan mencari nafkah. Artinya, secara fisik dan mental, mereka sudah dianggap "dewasa muda".

Teknik berkomunikasi antara anak *pra-baligh* dengan anak yang *sudah baligh* tentu tidaklah sama. Mengapa? Karena ada perbedaan level berpikir yang signifikan di antara keduanya.

Kenapa Cara Komunikasi Harus Berubah? (Why)

An adolescent no longer simply imitates (imitator). They are currently building what is known in Islam as **Islamic Akliah** (Islamic thinking pattern) and **Islamic Nafsiah** (Islamic emotional pattern). The foundation of their thinking is their belief. They start asking questions like "Why do I need to pray?" instead of just "How do I pray?"

If we continue to use the childlike language that dictates everything, adolescent children will feel undervalued. They need Islamic solutions to their life problems, and they need halal-haram standards as their own behavioral benchmarks.

How to Effectively Communicate? (How)

Based on Ustazah Yanti Tanjung's presentation, there are 5 main points we must apply as parents to ensure our messages reach their hearts without creating a distance:

1. Use Soft Words to Touch Their Hearts

Consistency is key. Address them with their favorite nickname. Avoid nicknames that demean or diminish their mental state. Use soft, pleasant-to-hear words that can touch their emotional side. Remember, adolescent children have sensitive emotions due to hormonal changes.

2. Cultivate Discussions and Seeking Opinions

Do not be an authoritarian parent. If there are important matters in the family, especially involving their child, invite them to sit and discuss. Ask for their opinions. "According to the younger one, how about we do this? ". This approach makes the child feel acknowledged and valued as an adult.

3. Use the Socratic Method

Instead of directly confronting them, try asking. "How did you feel at school today?", "What do you think about that incident?". Using the Socratic method helps us understand their internal responses without making them feel interrogated.

4. Honesty, Clarity, and Logic

Be honest and straightforward. Adolescents are capable of understanding logical arguments. Present your arguments clearly and easily understandable. Do not hide anything with the excuse "you are still young", as they no longer feel young.

5. Use Religious Language

This is most important. Connect every activity to Allah. Say "study so you can be smart" instead of saying "study so you can be smart". Instead, say "study because that is Allah's commandment so that we can benefit the community". In this way, the child's thinking will always be guided by a solid Islamic belief.

Closing: Cultivating Patience Through Dialogue

Learning from Prophet Ismail, his response was exceptionally extraordinary: "O my father, do what is commanded of you. Allah willing, you will bring me along with those who are patient." Such responses only come from healthy, honest, and belief-based communication over many years.

Parenting is not about controlling your child, but guiding them to be able to control themselves in front of Allah. Let's change the way we speak, so they become strong in their faith and gentle in their nature.
