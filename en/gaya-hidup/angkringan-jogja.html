About Angkringan

Angkringan is a traditional Yogyakarta warung, known for its street food stalls, usually operating at night. The name "angkring" comes from the word "angkring," meaning the wooden cart where vendors sell their food. This warung has become a favorite gathering place for young people, students, and the general public to enjoy affordable meals in a cozy and warm atmosphere.

What makes Angkringan so special is not just the taste of its food, but also the atmosphere and experience it offers. Here, you can feel the unique warmth of friendship in Yogyakarta, where people from diverse backgrounds can gather and enjoy food at very affordable prices.

The payment system at Angkringan is also unique - typically using a "honesty box" system where customers count their own consumed food and pay based on what they ate. This reflects the still preserved trust and honesty in Yogyakarta's culture.
