🕊️ Jangan Mudah Menghakimi Orang Lain: Belajar Menjaga Hati dan Lisan dalam Islam

📌 Introduction: Why We Should Not Be Easy Judges of Others?

Hello, friends! I'm sure among us, many of you have judged someone without even realizing it, based on their outward appearance or a short story. Maybe you saw a neighbor who rarely goes to the mosque or a friend who is busy with work during Ramadan. Subconsciously, you might have muttered to yourself, *"Why aren't they praying? Why are they so busy working?"* But we don't know the whole struggle they are facing. This topic is very close to us: **don't be easy judges of others**. I will invite you to talk casually but seriously about the dangers of suudzoon, the importance of husnuzan, and how to maintain a pure heart and speech according to Islam. We'll use the 5W+1H approach to make it clearer. Let's start!

📏 Habits of Judging Others Based on Our Own Size

Kita tend to use our personal experiences as a universal standard. For instance, if we regularly pray tarawih, we hope that others will do so too. If we have free time, we wonder why others are always busy. However, each human being has a background, life burdens, trials, and different levels of mental strength. Forgetting this often leads us to hastily judge without any basis. I myself still fall into this sometimes, which is why I want to learn more deeply.

🔍 The Real World Is Not Always As It Appears

Imagine someone who still works at night during Ramadhan. We see them as busy, then our minds interpret: "They are too attached to the world." However, it could be that they are struggling to pay off debts, support their families, or even hold back their sadness due to not being able to perform the rituals fully. That's where understanding the importance of **not easily judging others** becomes crucial, as we don't see the whole story. Maybe behind their sweat and effort, there are tears that aren't visible.

⚠️ The Danger of Prejudice in Islam

In Islam, prejudiced thinking (suudzon) is not a minor issue. Prejudiced thinking damages the heart, social relationships, and even good deeds. It often serves as the gateway to gossip, slander, and hatred. The Prophet ﷺ warned us to stay away from prejudiced thinking, as it is considered impure speech. I ponder how many conflicts start from unverified assumptions.

📖 Verses from the Qur'an on the Prohibition of Prejudiced Thinking

Allah Ta’ala says in Surah Al-Hujurat verse 12: *“O believers, be careful of (guarding) your duty to Allah, and listen not, nor speak, save that which is good…”* This verse is clear: wrong suspicions are not just social errors, but also sins. Meaning, when we forget to be skeptical, we are risking the purity of our own hearts.

🗣️ Hadith on Guarding the Tongue and Heart

The Prophet ﷺ said: *“Whoever believes in Allah and the Last Day, let him speak only that which is good or be silent.”* (Sahih Bukhari). Judging often starts from the tongue. If the tongue is guarded, the heart follows suit. I remember, how light are words yet heavy their weight in the afterlife.

🌙 Ramadhan and Social Empathy Test

Ramadan is not just about personal worship. It is also about social empathy. We might be able to pray tarawih in full, recite Quran every night, do i’tikaf. But some of our brothers and sisters still have to work late shifts, take care of patients, or drive for income. Are they lacking in faith? Probably not. Maybe they are fighting the way of Allah in their own ways. Therefore, **do not easily judge others**, especially during the month of boundless mercy.

❓ Why We Often Judge Quickly?

  • Ego feels better than others.
  • Lack of empathy to understand others' positions.
  • Incomplete information leading to hasty conclusions.
  • Comparing oneself with others.

When the heart is not trained to be humble, it easily feels the most justified.

💔 The Spiritual Impact of Judging Others

Mentraping orang lain can erase one's merits without realizing, fostering subtle pride, reducing one's sensitivity, and distancing oneself from Allah's mercy. Ironically, we are busy critiquing others when our own selves are often not clean from shortcomings. I feel this is a stern warning to keep introspection.

✨ Mengganti Suudzon dengan Husnuzan

✨ Replace Faults with Virtues

Islam teaches virtues (berprasangka baik). When seeing something doubtful, habitually consider: "*'Maybe there's an unknown reason,' 'Maybe they're struggling,' 'Maybe I haven't understood yet.'*" This way, the heart becomes calmer and life more peaceful.

📜 A Muslim's Etiquette in Evaluating Others

Islamic etiquette teaches: not to evaluate someone's intentions, not to comment on the unknown, and to prioritize clarification before concluding. Natures are within one's heart, and only Allah knows the heart. This principle must be held firmly.

🪞 Engage in Self-Reflection Instead of Critiquing Others

Instead of being busy evaluating others, better to ask oneself: "*'Have my prayers been pure? Have my intentions been clean? Have I helped those in need?'*" Self-reflection makes us aware that our own selves are still full of shortcomings. I myself always try to meditate every night, maybe to understand someone's wounded heart due to my words.

If seeing someone in hardship or lack, the best choice is to pray for goodness, ask for ease, and hope for their improvement. Prayer does not hurt. Suffering does, even if only in the heart. I prefer to pray, because prayer is the weapon of a believer.

🔍 Allah Knows the Heart Best

Allah knows what comes to mind, what is hidden in the heart, and what is not said. This serves as a strong reminder to be cautious. Sometimes we feel no guilt because we did not say it, but our heart already condemned. Truly, Allah is the best guardian.

✅ Practical Ways to Train the Heart to Not Easily Judge Others

  • Delay judgment, don't rush to assess.
  • Seek full information, clarify if needed.
  • Remember that we also have hidden faults to cover.
  • Practise praying for goodness for others.
  • Train empathy by imagining oneself in their position.

The more we train ourselves, the softer our hearts become.

🍃 Don't Easily Judge Others: A Humble Attitude

When we truly understand that life is not always as it appears on the surface, we will be more cautious. We realize that every person has their wounds, trials, and struggles. Their struggles might be heavier than we imagine. A humble attitude makes us less likely to pass judgment.

🔚 Conclusion: Becoming a More Gentle Muslim

Pada the end, **do not easily judge others**, not only social advice. It is a spiritual practice. A practice for cleansing the heart, guarding the tongue, strengthening empathy, and enhancing humility. If you cannot help, do not add burdens. If you cannot strengthen, do not weaken. If you do not know the whole story, do not conclude. Because Allah has the power to reverse the situation of anyone He desires. Today we feel better, tomorrow we could be in the same position. Let's keep our hearts and tongues, so that our lives are more peaceful and blessed.

❓ FAQ

1. Why shouldn't we easily judge others?

Because what is seen is not necessarily a reflection of someone's entire life. It could be due to reasons or tests we do not know about.

2. What are the negative impacts of judging others?

Damaging the heart, fostering arrogance, damaging social relationships, and could be a sin if done out of bad suspicions.

4. Bagaimana cara melatih agar tidak mudah menghakimi?

Suudzon is a bad suspicion, while husnuzan is a good suspicion. Islam instructs us to be husnuzan.

4. How can one train not to easily judge?

By delaying conclusions, seeking complete information, remembering one's own mistakes, practicing prayer, and training empathy.

5. Is suspicion considered a sin?

Yes, some suspicion is considered a sin, as stated in QS Al-Hujurat verse 12.

6. What should be done when seeing someone do something apparently wrong?

Hold back judgment, seek clarification if necessary, and pray for their good. Do not judge hastily.

7. How does Islam view people who like to judge?

Islam forbids it because it can disrupt the bond of community and is a morally reprehensible behavior. What is recommended is mutual comfort with gentleness and praying for them.
