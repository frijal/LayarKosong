3 February 2026Opinion | Law | Discussion MK Rejects Mixed Religious Marriage Appeal: Why Do We Still Need Legal Certainty?

A major development in our legal world has occurred. The Constitutional Court (MK) recently delivered a firm blow. They officially dismissed the material appeal regarding the challenge to the prohibition of mixed religious marriage. What does this mean? The provision in Law No. 1 of 1974 on Marriage still stands strong like a coral stone.

Marriage in this country is not just a matter of "I love you, let's get married." In Indonesia, this sacred promise is the meeting point between state law, religious norms, and social contracts. Let's dissect this further to understand why this ruling was made and its implications for us who live in this multicultural land.

### 5W+1H: Unraveling the MK Decision

**What (What Happened?):** The MK dismissed the appeal submitted by the applicant seeking legal legitimacy for a mixed religious marriage. The MK deemed the reasons for the appeal unclear or "infringed upon." The judges felt that the issue raised was more about administrative registration, rather than constitutional violations at the core of the law.

What is this decision about and what does it mean for us who still live in this multicultural nation? Let's explore these questions in-depth.

**Who (Who is Involved?):** Besides the applicant and the MK judge, this decision involves all Indonesian citizens. Behind it, there are millions of couples who might be at a crossroads, as well as religious leaders who have consistently maintained the institution of marriage in accordance with their respective beliefs.

**Where (Di mana dampaknya terasa?):** Dampaknya terasa di Kantor Urusan Agama (KUA), Kantor Catatan Sipil, hingga ke ruang-ruang diskusi di media sosial. Tidak ada celah hukum baru yang dibuka, sehingga prosedur di seluruh wilayah NKRI tetap seragam.

**Where (Where is the impact felt?):** The impact is felt in the Religious Affairs Office (KUA), Civil Affairs offices, all the way down to the discussion rooms in social media. There is no new legal loophole opened, so procedures across the entire Indonesian territory remain uniform.

**Why (Mengapa ini diputuskan?):** Alasannya fundamental, Mas. Hakim MK memandang bahwa Pasal 2 Ayat (1) UU Perkawinan adalah kunci. Keabsahan pernikahan diserahkan kepada agama masing-masing. Karena semua agama resmi di Indonesia memiliki pakem tersendiri soal ini, MK memilih untuk tidak "mengintervensi" wilayah teologis tersebut.

**When (When does this decision take effect?):** The MK decision is final and binding upon reading. This ends the long speculation that momentarily heated up at the beginning of 2026.

Perspectively: Not Just a Contract Letter

Illibarly, from a sociological standpoint, this regulation is not meant to arbitrarily undermine human rights. Rather, its purpose is to maintain balance. Imagine if a country enforced a standard that clashed with values deeply embedded in society over hundreds of years. Instead of fostering order, what results is social chaos.

Religion has already established the framework for a good life. If two people of faith live together, their viewpoints, educational approaches, and even the distribution of assets are usually based on similar principles. If the fundamental principles themselves differ, the foundation of their households is at risk when future differences in perspectives hit them.

Key Points You Should Know:

  • **Islamic Law:** For Muslims, a marriage between people of different faiths remains legally invalid/haram according to the majority of scholars and Islamic law in Indonesia.
  • **Religious Authority:** The validity of the marriage remains dependent on the religious laws of each individual, not the personal preference of the party seeking the marriage.
  • **State Role:** The state only records marriages officially recognized by religion.
  • **Status Quo:** Law No. 12 of 1974 on Marriage still stands without any revisions.
