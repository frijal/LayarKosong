⚖️ Scholar Opinions on the Shariah of Sleeping After Ashar

Explanation by Shaikh Muhammad Shalih Al-Munajjid

He stated in his fatwa: *"Sleeping after the Maghrib prayer is permissible (mubah). There is no authentic hadith prohibiting it."* I fully agree, as this provides comfort for the community.

Fatwa of Al-Lajnah Ad-Daimah

The Saudi Commission for Scholarly Fatwa (Al-Lajnah) also emphasized that there is no issue in sleeping after Ashar, as there is no evidence prohibiting or mandating it. Thus, from a legal perspective, the door is wide open.

🌙 During Ramadan, Can One Sleep After Ashar? Permissible or Mubah

Answer: **permissible (mubah), even more so**. Ramadan does not change the original legal status. But I note a few important points: do not miss Maghrib, do not neglect the night prayer out of laziness, and do not make it a habit that harms health. Islam is a religion that makes things easy, not difficult.

🩺 Medical Perspective on Sleeping After Ashar

Sleep-Wake Rhythm Disturbances

Secularly, sleeping after Dhuhr is not dangerous as long as it is brief. However, if done every day until Dhuha, it can disrupt the circadian rhythm (the body's biological clock). As a result: difficulty sleeping at night, being late for prayers, and missing Fajr prayers. I experienced this firsthand, and it indeed lowers productivity.

The Importance of Night Sleep

Night sleep is the primary time when the body regenerates cells, repairs the immune system, and consolidates memories. Daytime naps cannot replace it. Therefore, while sleeping after Dhuhr is permissible, we must still prioritize sufficient night sleep.

☀️ Qailulah: Prophet’s Sunnah Recommended

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Qailulah (sleep) for indeed the devils do not sleep." (Reported by Thabrani, graded as authentic by Al-Albani). I was inspired, it turns out that proper daytime sleep actually earns rewards.

Ideal Duration of Qailulah

Scholars and health experts agree that ideal qailulah is 20-60 minutes. Avoid longer durations as they can cause dizziness (sleep inertia). I personally practice this, and am grateful for a refreshed body to perform Maghrib prayers.

📖 Time of Sleep in the Quran

Allah states in Surah Ar-Ruum verse 23: "And among the signs of His dominion is that He causes you to sleep at night and by day." I ponder, this verse actually appreciates daytime sleep as a blessing. Thus, sleeping after Dhuhr, as long as it is not excessive, is considered daytime sleep.

⚠️ Risks of Sleeping After Dhuhr Too Long

  • Disrupts night sleep, leading to staying up late.
  • Being late for Fajr prayers and missing the jama’ah Fajr prayers.
  • Menurunking the quality of worship because the body is weak.
  • Distracted concentration the next day.

This doesn't mean it's haram, but it's not wise. Personally, I limit afternoon sleep to 15-30 minutes to stay fresh until Magrib.

📝 Tips for Managing Sleep During Ramadan

  1. Perform qailulah around 20-30 minutes before Dzuhur or after that.
  2. If you're very tired after Dzuhur, take a power nap for 10-20 minutes (use a timer to avoid falling asleep).
  3. Set a nighttime sleep time, such as 21:30-22:00, consistently.
  4. Avoid caffeine and gadgets before sleep.
  5. Wake up for suhoor calmly, don't rush.

I've tried these tips and fortunately, the worship is better maintained.

**What:** Sleeping after Dzuhur during Ramadan.
**Why:** To recover from fasting fatigue.
**Who:** Muslims observing Ramadan.
**When:** Between Dzuhur and Magrib.
**Where:** At home or a resting place.

❓ FAQ: Is Sleeping After Dzuhur During Ramadan Invalidating Fasting?

1. Does sleeping after Dzuhur invalidate fasting?

No, sleeping does not invalidate fasting at all. What invalidates fasting are eating, drinking, and other specified actions.

2. Is it considered makruh?

Majority of scholars consider it not makruh, as there's no authentic evidence prohibiting it. Its hukum is mubah/allowed.

3. Does it cause a loss of reason?

No evidence of medical or authentic hadith. This is a myth stemming from false hadith.

4. Which is better, qailulah (sleep before Zuhr) or sleep after Ashar?

Qailulah is more important as a sunnah and more supportive of nocturnal prayers. However, sleeping after Ashar is allowed if necessary.

5. Is it sinful to frequently sleep in the afternoon until Maghrib?

No, it is not sinful, but it can harm oneself by missing out on productive time and potentially disrupting the congregation for Maghrib.

6. What if I am very tired and afraid I won't be able to perform tahil?

It is permissible to take a brief nap with the intention of restoring energy, as long as it doesn't extend too late and you still strive to wake up for tahil. May it be with good intention, may Allah reward it.

7. Are there any specific recommendations from the scholars regarding sleep during Ramadan?

Scholars recommend regulating rest times to maximize worship, including utilizing qailulah and avoiding long nights without benefit.

**Summary:** Waking Up in Ramadan: Is Sleeping After Ashar Permitted?

The answer from a legal perspective is **permissible (mubah)**, as there is no authentic evidence prohibiting it. However, from a medical and worship management perspective, we are encouraged to be judicious: prioritize qailulah, limit afternoon naps, and maintain high-quality nighttime sleep. Islam is balanced, not overly restrictive or indulgent. I hope this clarifies any misconceptions that have arisen. May you have a blessed and healthy Ramadan! 🤲
